About Minecraft

Minecraft is known as a sandbox game because it has no set goal,
allowing players endless possibilities to create, explore, and interact with the world as they choose.

A core mechanic of the game involves breaking various blocks, gathering resources,
and using them to craft new items, tools, and build imaginative structures.

Survival and Exploration: Players can explore vast landscapes, manage resources like hunger, battle hostile
creatures (mobs), and navigate challenges in different game modes, most notably Survival and Hardcore modes.

The Creator Notch

Notch developed the initial version of Minecraft (originally called "Cave Game") during a single weekend in May 2009,
inspired by games like Infiniminer and Dwarf Fortress. He then released the game on an indie developer forum,
where it quickly gained a dedicated community and grew in popularity through player feedback and word-of-mouth.

As Minecraft's popularity exploded, Notch founded Mojang Studios in 2010 to support the game's development.
In 2014, he sold the company and the rights to Minecraft to Microsoft for a reported $2.5 billion,
making him a billionaire.

After the sale, Persson left Mojang and transferred creative control to Jens Bergensten, stating he didn't
want the responsibility of managing such a massive operation. Since his departure, his controversial social
media posts regarding various social issues have led to Microsoft largely distancing itself from him and
removing most of his name references from the game.
